AI-Powered Dental X-Ray Diagnosis

Track: Foreign-Trained Dentist – Research Fellowship

Background

Dental radiographs are vital for diagnosing conditions such as caries, bone loss, periapical lesions, and impacted teeth. However, manual interpretation is subjective and inconsistent. Artificial Intelligence (AI) has the potential to enhance accuracy and streamline clinical decisions.

Objective

To build a deep learning model capable of:

  • Detecting dental pathologies in 2D X-rays
  • Assessing severity of conditions
  • Recommending treatment plans based on clinical best practices

Methods

Using 5,000–10,000 labeled radiographs, we’ll train a multi-task CNN using YOLOv5 for object detection and ResNet/EfficientNet for classification. Training will include data augmentation and optimization via focal loss. We will evaluate model performance using mAP, sensitivity, specificity, and AUC metrics.

Validation & Usability

A clinical usability study will be conducted with dentists, measuring diagnostic speed, user trust, and AI-aided decision accuracy through surveys and time trials.

Expected Outcomes

The model aims for ≥85% sensitivity and ≥80% specificity across key conditions. The tool will deliver real-time visual insights and streamline dental treatment planning.

Impact

This system has the potential to reduce diagnostic error, optimize clinical workflows, and bring AI innovation into day-to-day dental care—especially valuable in underserved or high-volume practices.
